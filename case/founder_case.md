# Founder Case Simulation

## Problem

Revenue growth has slowed over the last 30 days.  
The founder suspects issues in the funnel but lacks clarity on where exactly the drop-offs are happening.

---

## Approach

1. Break down the funnel into key stages:
   - Lead → Demo Booked → Demo Attended → Converted

2. Measure conversion rates at each stage

3. Identify the stage with the highest drop-off

4. Quantify revenue leakage

5. Prioritize actions based on impact

---

## Key Findings

- Demo Booking Rate: ~58%
- Demo Attendance Rate: ~50%
- Conversion Rate: ~18%

### Insight

- Largest drop-off observed at **conversion stage**
- However, **attendance is the highest leverage point** for improving overall revenue

---

## Root Cause Hypothesis

### Conversion Issues
- Weak sales pitch
- Poor objection handling
- Lack of urgency in closing

### Attendance Issues
- No reminders
- Long delay between booking and demo
- Low intent from leads

---

## Recommended Actions

### Immediate (High Priority)
- Implement WhatsApp + email reminders
- Reduce delay between booking and demo
- Enable easy rescheduling

### Short Term
- Improve demo script
- Train sales team on objection handling
- Introduce urgency triggers (limited offers)

---

## Execution Plan

| Action | Owner | Timeline |
|--------|------|--------|
| WhatsApp reminders | Ops | 3 days |
| Reduce scheduling delay | Product/Ops | 5 days |
| Sales training | Sales Lead | 7 days |

---

## Expected Outcome

- Increase attendance rate → +10–15%
- Improve conversion → +5–10%
- Revenue uplift driven by higher throughput across funnel

---

## Final Take

Improving conversion is important, but fixing attendance first creates a multiplier effect across the funnel.  
Focus on upstream leverage before downstream optimization.
